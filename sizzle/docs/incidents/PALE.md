## 0. Design Spine (read first)

- **Convergent branching.** Choices and checks reroute *texture* — how cleanly she reads the rule, how close the gaze comes to catching her, how hard the command bites when it lands — never the outcome. Every path ends: she works out the loophole, commits to an irrevocable strike, the woman says **"STOP,"** momentum carries the blow through, the woman drops, the compulsion releases, the Branch is already on its way. A failed Composure hold = the command lands with full force and she nearly obeys — but the swing is already past recall. A beat, never a fail state.
- **Felt stakes, fixed plumbing.** The player should believe one wrong glance ends her. In story terms it can't. In *who she becomes* terms it can — see §8 skill grant.
- **Pre-Branch voice.** August 2003. No NYSE vocabulary, no Branch frame. She has whatever her background gives her (a cop's threat-reading, an analyst's pattern sense, a psych grad's behavioural eye, an outsider's plain dread) — never "command hazard" or "vector." Those words arrive only in the Branch report at the end.
- **She is not special — and the ending proves it.** This is the load-bearing rule of this incident. She does **not** resist the command through willpower. When the woman says "STOP," it lands; she feels the iron certainty that she must obey. She wins **only** because she committed to a physical action whose momentum could no longer be recalled. She cheated the *timing* of the rule, not its force. Any version where she "shakes it off" or is too strong-willed to obey is wrong and breaks the whole point.
- **The PC is sex-positive — in every background, in every incident.** (Cross-cutting; see MANITOULIN §0.) The tableau is sexually explicit and humiliating, and her horror is the **commanded obedience against will**, not the sex itself. She is not disgusted that strangers are doing sexual things; she is terrified that a sentence spoken with a look has turned people into furniture and they cannot refuse. Never frame her fear as prudishness.
- **The victims are people, not a joke.** The objectified adults — a footrest, a fruit-bearer, a man whose erection has become a coat-hook for an umbrella — are rendered with humanity that surfaces when the command breaks. The horror is the obedience; the humiliation is theirs, not the reader's entertainment.
- **The woman is not a vamp.** She is detached, neutral, inhumanly casual — she gives absolute commands in the flat tone of someone reading a grocery list. No dominatrix, no succubus, no theatrical villainy. Her pale eyes are *memorable and wrong*, not monstrous. The horror is the mismatch: ordinary speech, absolute human obedience.
- **Sell the dread, don't police the heat.** *(Cross-cutting — true for the whole game.)* Our job is to make the horror land: the overwritten consent, the obedience against will, the humiliation done *to* people. We do **not** write to suppress or correct for arousal. If the explicit tableau also reads as charged to a given player, that's their interpretation and it's fine. Aim at the dread; don't aim away from the heat.
- **The PC can be funny; the situation never is.** *(Cross-cutting.)* The incident, the woman, and the commanded victims are never played for laughs — the obedience is real and horrifying. But the **player character's voice** can be dry, sardonic, gallows-wry: a woman who copes through clipped, mordant interior observation. Her wit is character and relief valve, never a joke at a victim's expense.

### NYSE grammar (how this differs from the first two)

The Blackout was an *environmental* anomaly (solved by **adding** light). Manitoulin was a *focal-marking* anomaly (solved by **destroying** the pattern). The Pale Eyes is a **rule hazard** — a precise, person-anchored mechanism with a stated condition (direct eye contact) and a stated effect (her spoken word becomes absolute truth/command). It isn't beaten by adding or breaking an object. It's beaten by **exploiting the rule's own latency**: the command needs the gaze *and* a beat to take hold, so an action already irreversibly in motion completes before the word can stop it. The verb here is **outpace / commit**, not *add* or *break*. Keep the three grammatically distinct so the incidents don't read as "weird shit happened" three times.

## 1. Playable Sequence Premise

August 2003, downtown Ottawa, a hotel lobby in a hard summer downpour — wet coats, dripping umbrellas, water sheeting down the glass, the white noise of rain pulling everything quiet. The player character is here for her own reasons (background-dependent) and is crossing the lobby when she notices a lounge alcove off the main flow where the air has gone wrong: people too still, an arrangement too orderly, no one talking. At its centre, served and comfortable, sits a woman with pale eyes who speaks to people in a flat, bored voice — and they obey her absolutely. One is her footrest. One feeds her cut fruit. A man stands rigid with her umbrella hooked on him. The player character grasps the rule by watching it work: *the woman's word becomes law, but only when her eyes meet yours.* The whole sequence is the discipline of not being looked at — using cover, sightlines, a mirror (useful, but lethal if the reflected gaze finds her). She can't out-stare her and can't out-argue her. She works out the only move the rule can't survive: strike without meeting the eyes, commit to a blow already unstoppable before the woman can say a word. She swings a fire extinguisher (or a chair, or a luggage cart) from a blind angle. The woman's gaze snaps to her. **"STOP."** It lands — for one impossible second she *knows* she must obey — but the swing is already past recall. The woman drops. The room comes back to itself, ashamed and confused. And the people who arrive to take the woman away are calm, fast, and not police.

**One-line for the player:** *You spent the whole time learning not to look at her — and then you won by swinging at something you couldn't afford to see.*

## 3. Opening Setup & Background Variants

**Universal frame (PALE-085 → PALE-105):** the same three beats of normality (a reason to be in Ottawa, the storm breaking, ducking into the lobby), the same downpour, the same crossing of the lobby toward the wrong-feeling alcove. The variants change *why she's in that lobby* and *what her eyes are trained to catch* — not the events. (Per the Blackout/Manitoulin rule: one shared anchor location, divergent reasons for being there.)

- **CSIS analyst — `City Dweller`:** The most natural fit — she's in the hotel for work, training, or an intelligence-analysis event (Ottawa is home turf for the trade). Desk-trained, not field-trained: she *infers* the rule from pattern — who obeys, when, the precondition of the look — before she's consciously decided she's investigating. Her edge is analysis; she reads the mechanism fastest.
- **RCMP constable — `Northern Ontario`:** In Ottawa for a **policing conference** (this origin puts the conference in Ottawa, not Toronto). She reads the alcove as a threat scene: count the people, who's in distress, where's the exit, what's the principal doing. Her eye is for control and coercion, so the *obedience* registers as wrong to her before the *sex* does.
- **Grad student (psychology) — `City Dweller`, `Lived in Toronto`:** In Ottawa on a personal trip/vacation. She reads it as *behaviour* — compliance, learned helplessness, authority response — and has real pre-Branch academic vocabulary for it, which makes the moment it exceeds those rules more frightening, not less.
- **Unemployed after university — `City Dweller`, `Lived in Toronto`:** In Ottawa on a personal trip. The outsider with no professional frame — the most viscerally exposed, fewest defenses; her edge is stubborn nerve and noticing, not training.

**Voice discipline:** same woman, different prior years. One or two sentences of shading per branch, parallel structure. All four share the not-being-seen spine; they differ only in what her attention is trained to do.

## 4. Escalation Pattern

There's no traversal here — she stays in and around one alcove. The escalation is built from three rising pressures that climb together:

**Comprehension (what she understands):** wrong feeling → orderly-but-wrong arrangement → the woman → *the rule itself* (eye contact + word = obedience) → the loophole (latency can be outpaced). Each step is more dangerous to *know*, because understanding draws her closer.

**Proximity / exposure (how close the gaze comes):** observed from across the lobby → from cover → through a mirror (a step closer, a real risk) → a near-miss as the gaze sweeps → committing inside arm's reach without looking. The closer she needs to get to act, the harder it is to stay unseen.

**The single deep contact (the spike):** unlike Manitoulin's progressive soak, her exposure is *near-zero by design* — the whole game is avoiding contact — until **one** command lands at PALE-160. That one hit is the entire dose, and it's deep: absolute obedience, for a second, in her body. The structure (low ambient, one spike) is itself distinct from the other incidents and should drive the `$nyse.influence` shape (§8).

**Eroticism + horror:** the explicitness is in the *commanded* tableau, not in the PC. The wrongness of obedience is what must always be underneath — we aim at that dread, not away from any heat a player may read on top of it (§0). **Keep the rule strictly as the woman's mechanism — the narrator never endorses the obedience as deserved or natural** (STYLE-GUIDE §13.5).

## 5. Player Choices & Skill Checks

All convergent. Difficulties low-to-moderate; failure is always survivable and generative.

### Choices
- **PALE-125 — Mirror or cover.** *Mirror* → she learns the rule precisely and early (and the reflected-gaze danger is dramatized). *Cover/glimpses* → she pieces it together slower, more by inference. Same understanding by PALE-145; different route and self-image (clinical vs. cautious).
- **PALE-140 — Slip away or stop it.** *Slip away* → the open floor would put her in the gaze, or she gets two steps and can't leave them like that; she turns back. *Stop it* → straight to planning. Same destination; different framing (trapped into it vs. choosing it).
- **PALE-150 — What she swings (cosmetic).** Fire extinguisher / lobby chair / luggage cart / brass stanchion-and-rope. All produce "a heavy thing already in motion." Pure flavor; the object can colour the strike's texture at PALE-165.

### Skill checks (use `<<skillCheck>>`, onward links in both branches)
- **PALE-120 — Read the rule (Agent or Academic, low DC).** *Pass:* she names the precondition cleanly — *it's the eyes.* *Fail:* she gets it the hard way, a half-step too slow, a near-brush with the gaze; +texture, slightly more exposed. Both continue.
- **PALE-135 — Stay unseen / don't meet the eyes (Composure or Agent, moderate DC).** *Pass:* she breaks sightline in time. *Fail:* the gaze grazes her — not a full lock, but the pull of being *almost* addressed; she ducks out of it. +1 `$nyse.influence`. Both → PALE-140.
- **PALE-145 — Reason the loophole (Academic or Streetwise, low DC, optional).** *Pass:* she *works it out* — the command needs the look and a beat; commit before either can land. *Fail:* she doesn't reason it; instinct/desperation puts the same plan in her hands (attack from where she can't be seen). Both → PALE-150. The "ingenuity vs. luck" fork; both recruitment-worthy; differs only in prose texture and which skill earns the +1 (§8).
- **PALE-160 — The command hold (Composure, higher DC). The spine check.** *Pass:* she's so committed that "STOP" barely catches the edge of her; the follow-through is clean. *Fail:* the command lands like iron — she feels the absolute *must* and nearly freezes — but the object is already past the point of recall and connects anyway. **Never a loss: the blow lands on every path because it is physically unrecallable; the check only sets how hard the obedience bit and what it leaves behind.** +1 `$nyse.influence` on fail.

## 6. The Recruitment-Worthy Intervention

She defeats a person whose word is law — **without ever being stronger-willed than the command.** The ingenuity is entirely in the timing: she understands that the rule has a precondition (the gaze) and a latency (the beat between word and obedience), and she builds an action that finishes *before* either can apply to her. She strikes from an angle where she doesn't have to look, committing to a swing she physically cannot stop. When "STOP" lands, it lands fully — and is simply too late.

The Branch's read (surfaced only in the PALE-200/205 report documents, clinical voice): a civilian who, under a live compliance hazard, decoded its operating condition and defeated it by exploiting physical irreversibility rather than by resisting it — and who is the sole coherent account afterward. That combination — *understood the rule, beat it with physics, not willpower* — is the recruitment-worthy quality. It also tells the Branch something useful and a little chilling: she is not immune. She just thinks fast enough to make immunity unnecessary.

## 7. Aftermath & Branch / Robert Bridge

- **PALE-165 / PALE-170 (scene aftermath):** the woman drops; the flat voice cuts out; the commanded surface releases. People surface confused and ashamed — the umbrella clatters off the man, the fruit spills, someone covers themselves, someone weeps. Their personhood returns in specific human detail. The PC does not get thanked; she gets a room full of people who don't understand what just happened to them, and one woman unconscious on the floor.
- **PALE-175 (Branch on scene — distinct from the other incidents):** because this is Ottawa, the people who come are *already close*. Calm, quick, unbadged or wrongly-badged, plainly not hotel security and not city police. They take the woman. **She is smart and she knows something is up** — she reads the response as organized, practised, and pre-positioned (they arrived too fast and too certain to be reacting cold), even though she has no name for who they are. They ask her a few quiet, precise questions and tell her to go home. **No in-person Robert** — these are anonymous Branch personnel; the player should feel watched and dismissed at once, and should leave *certain* she stumbled into something with an apparatus behind it. This eerie fast-response is the incident's signature aftermath (vs. the days-later paperwork discovery of the Blackout/Manitoulin).
- **PALE-180 (interior aftermath):** the scar — the hidden-world fear (per INCIDENTPLAN), with this incident's specific residue: the bodily memory of absolute obedience, knowing for one impossible second that another person's sentence had become reality *inside her*. No narrow phobia.
- **PALE-200 / PALE-205 (Branch report documents, ~days later):** PoV-preserving, same as the siblings. PALE-200 is a field/containment occurrence extract — the woman as a "pale-eyed female subject," a "verbal compliance vector" *documented but not explained*, records removed, one coherent civilian account. PALE-205 is a recommendation memo signed Flett. Hands off to `CC-500 Summary`, which sets `$player.incitingIncident`. First in-person Robert beat is deferred to the 2005 INTRO briefing (consistent with the siblings; see §11). Note: Flett reviews the file; he was **not** on scene — the anonymous responders at PALE-175 are not him.

Robert relationship seed (keyed off `$player.incitingIncident`, no separate tag): he trusts her because she read an impossible rule under pressure and beat it with timing instead of bravado — and he quietly notes that she took a direct command and only survived it on a technicality, which is exactly the kind of luck he can't count on twice.

## 10. Risks, Content Warnings, Tonal Pitfalls

**Content warnings (this sequence):**
- Sexual humiliation and objectification under compulsion — people commanded into degrading sexual/servile arrangements against their will. The participants are **victims under command**, rendered with humanity. What must land is the wrongness of obedience; we sell the dread and don't police the heat (§0). If the explicit tableau also reads as charged to a given player, that's their interpretation and fine — the horror just has to be underneath it.
- The non-negotiable is that the **overwritten consent** (a spoken command becoming a person's reality) is always the load-bearing feeling. Reading *also* as charged is acceptable and player-dependent.
- Consent obliteration is the central horror — and uniquely literal here: a sentence becomes a person's reality.
- **No minors.** The commanded adults are an unambiguously adult mix of genders. No ambiguity in the prose.

> **Explicitness + placeholder convention (cross-cutting; see MANITOULIN §10).** The default is that the drafting agent **writes the tableau (PALE-130) and the commanded acts very explicitly** — this is the explicit heart of the sequence and should not be softened. The placeholder block is a **fallback only**: drop it in *solely if the drafting agent itself judges a specific beat to need more explicitness than it can comfortably write* (the umbrella-on-erection image is the likeliest candidate). If used, keep the beat structure, links, and variable hooks intact around it, e.g.:
>
> ```
> /* %%% HUMAN-DRAFT %%%
>    PALE-130 — the commanded arrangement, full view.
>    Intent: explicit + humiliating; victims' humanity intact; horror = obedience,
>    NOT the sex; PC's reaction is dread at overwritten consent, never prudishness. ~110 words.
>    %%% END %%% */
> ```
>
> **State the specifics in this design doc, not euphemisms.** If a beat-map note can only gesture ("a humiliating arrangement") instead of naming what is depicted, that vagueness propagates into the prose and pinpoints the beat a human may need to draft. The explicit content for PALE-130 is therefore stated concretely in §2 (footrest, fruit-bearer, the umbrella hooked on a man's erection, held rigid) rather than gestured at.

**Tonal pitfalls (from INCIDENTPLAN + this design):**
- **Don't make her immune or strong-willed.** The whole point is that the command *works* on her and she wins on physics. If she resists "STOP" by force of will, the incident is broken.
- **Don't make the woman a vamp/domme/succubus.** Detached, neutral, casual. Pale eyes memorable and wrong, not monstrous. The horror is the flat-voiced ordinariness of absolute command.
- **Don't make the victims a joke.** The humiliation is theirs and it's horror, not comedy or spectacle. Dignity returns when the command breaks.
- **Don't frame her fear as prudishness** (sex-positivity rule, §0). Her dread is the overwritten will, never the sex.
- **Keep the rule strictly the woman's mechanism.** The narrator never endorses the obedience as natural or deserved (STYLE-GUIDE §13.5).
- **Mirrors are consistently dangerous.** A reflected gaze counts the same as a direct one. Don't let a mirror be a "safe" cheat; it buys observation at real risk.
- **Don't use the "smart-home assistant" comparison in prose** — it's anachronistic for 2003 and design-shorthand only (per INCIDENTPLAN). Convey the flat-command register through her actual diction and tone.
- **Don't let Branch vocabulary leak into the 2003 flashback.** Pre-Branch voice until the report passages.
